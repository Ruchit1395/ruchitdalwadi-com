## Important publishing rule

The first social surface should be native thought, not link.

Reason: the goal is reach. The essay is canonical, but the social post must earn distribution before asking people to leave the platform.

## Launch timing

Preferred windows:

- LinkedIn: Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday morning in the audience's primary timezone.
- X: same day, within 15 minutes of LinkedIn, or 2-3 hours later if the LinkedIn post needs focused reply time.

If launching immediately:

- Do not wait for the "perfect" time.
- Launch only if the next 60 minutes are available for replies.

## The 100k reach strategy in one sentence

Make the idea native enough to travel, then use comments and direct sends to put it in front of people whose reply/repost/comment can unlock second-degree distribution.

## What "good" looks like after 48 hours

Minimum viable traction:

- 2,000+ combined impressions,
- 10+ meaningful comments/replies,
- 3+ people in the target audience responding,
- 1+ direct message or email,
- at least one comment thread worth turning into a follow-up post.

Strong traction:

- 10,000+ combined impressions,
- 30+ meaningful comments/replies,
- 5+ reposts/shares by target-audience accounts,
- 3+ direct messages/emails,
- a clear winning angle for the next post.

Breakout path:

- A founder/investor/operator with relevant audience comments early.
- Ruchit replies with a thoughtful expansion.
- That comment thread becomes the actual distribution object.

## If the post underperforms

Do not delete.

Do this instead:

1. Pull the strongest sentence into a standalone post.
2. Change the opening from abstract claim to painful scenario.
3. Comment the sharper take under active conversations.
4. Send the essay to 10 better-matched people.

Underperformance usually means the hook or initial distribution target was wrong, not that the idea is dead.
