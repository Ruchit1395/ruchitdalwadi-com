# 30-day free reach strategy

## Goal

Reach 100,000 total viewers/impressions across X and LinkedIn in 30 days using one canonical essay as the content source.

This is an ambitious target, not a guaranteed outcome. The strategy maximizes odds by turning one essay into a campaign, not a one-off link drop.

## Campaign thesis

Core claim:

> The first ten customers of a B2B AI startup are not bought with marketing. They are recruited through founder-led, hand-built design partnerships.

Why this can travel:

- It contradicts the default "run ads, post content, hire sales" instinct.
- It names a painful stage: first 10 customers.
- It gives founders a concrete playbook.
- It gives investors something to forward to portfolio founders.
- It gives GTM/product operators something to debate.

## Channel roles

LinkedIn:

- Primary reach and inbound channel.
- Best for founders, operators, investors, PMs, and GTM people.
- Use one strong narrative post, then comment deeply on adjacent posts for 48 hours.
- If the anchor post underperforms, switch immediately to comments under posts that already have engagement. LinkedIn comments are a primary distribution surface, not a backup.

X:

- Discovery and idea-testing channel.
- Best for punchy contrarian claims, founder Twitter, AI builders, and operators.
- Use one thread plus three standalone posts. Reply/comment around the topic before and after posting.

Website:

- Canonical destination.
- Link only after the native post has earned attention, unless the platform context naturally rewards links.

Direct outreach:

- Not spam. Send only when the essay is clearly useful to the recipient.
- Target founders building B2B AI, investors with AI/GTM portfolio companies, and GTM/product operators who write about founder-led sales.

## Positioning

Do not frame this as "I wrote a blog."

Frame it as:

- "The first 10 AI customers are a design-partner problem."
- "The first AI sales motion is research disguised as revenue."
- "Free pilots are a trap. Charge small, then over-deliver."
- "Your first customers pay for founder attention, not software."

## Posting architecture

Day -1:

- Comment on 20 relevant LinkedIn posts.
- Reply to 20 relevant X posts.
- Do not promote the essay yet.
- Goal: warm the network around the topic.

Day 0:

- Publish LinkedIn anchor post.
- Publish X thread.
- Do not lead with the link in the first line.
- Add the canonical link as a first comment/reply after the post is live.
- Reply to every meaningful comment within the first hour.

Day +1:

- Publish standalone short post 1 on X.
- Publish standalone short post 1 or 2 on LinkedIn if the anchor post has slowed.
- Send 10 direct messages/emails to highly relevant people.
- Continue commenting around the topic.

Day +2:

- Publish standalone short post 2 on X.
- Send 10 more direct messages/emails.
- Comment on 20 more high-signal posts.

Day +3 to +7:

- Publish remaining short posts.
- Turn good replies into follow-up posts.
- Quote/reply to people discussing first customers, design partners, AI GTM, or founder-led sales.

Week 2 to 4:

- Repost the strongest idea in a new format every 4-5 days.
- Convert high-performing comments into standalone posts.
- If a founder/investor replies, ask one specific follow-up question to continue the thread.

## Daily operating cadence

Use `daily-operating-schedule.md` as the current day-to-day execution rhythm.

Baseline:

- X: 1 standalone native post per day.
- LinkedIn: 1 native post every 24-48 hours, roughly 3-4 per week.
- X replies: 2 batches per day, 8-12 replies each.
- LinkedIn comments: 2 batches per day, 5-8 comments each.
- Metrics: 2-3 read-only checks per day.
- Direct outreach: 5-10 specific people per day only when the essay is genuinely useful.

Do not treat volume as the win. The win is reply quality, second-degree distribution, and profile-view movement from the right rooms.

## 100k reach math

Target mix:

- LinkedIn anchor post: 20k-50k impressions if it catches second-degree distribution.
- X thread: 10k-30k impressions if picked up by founder/AI circles.
- Three short posts: 5k-15k each.
- Comments/replies: 10k-30k aggregate profile/post views.
- Direct shares from 20-30 targeted people: variable but potentially the unlock.

The unlock is not posting volume. It is getting the right 10-20 people to comment, repost, reply, or forward.

If the original posts are below pace, the best free lever is to comment where attention already exists:

- Find recent high-engagement posts from founders, operators, investors, and AI/GTM commentators.
- Add a useful missing lens with no link.
- Track replies and profile movement.
- Turn the strongest reply/comment thread into the next standalone post.

## Rules

- No fake engagement.
- No mass DM spam.
- No auto-replies.
- No AI-sounding generic comments.
- No "please share" unless there is a real relationship.
- Every comment must add a useful thought, counterpoint, or example.

## Success metrics

Primary:

- LinkedIn impressions.
- X impressions.
- Replies/comments from founders, operators, investors, and AI builders.

Secondary:

- Profile views.
- Website clicks.
- Email/contact clicks.
- New followers.
- Direct replies.
