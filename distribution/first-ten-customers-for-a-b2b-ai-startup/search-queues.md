- posts where comments are all low-quality,

## Pre-launch comment prompts

Use these before publishing. Do not include the essay link.

### Founder-led sales

The thing I would add: founder-led sales is not just about trust. It is about compression. The same person hears the objection, sees the workflow, owns the integration, and updates the product instinct before the next call.

### First customers

For the first 10 customers, I think the question is less "can we sell this?" and more "can this sale teach us something reusable?" Otherwise early revenue quietly becomes custom consulting.

### AI pilots

One trap with AI pilots: free pilots create polite feedback. Small paid pilots create honest feedback. The price can be low, but the customer needs to make a real decision.

### Design partners

Design partners only work if both sides agree that the learning can be generalized. Without that, the design partner becomes a custom branch with nicer language.

### Live workflow

My favorite test for early AI products: can the founder run the workflow live in the sales call on realistic customer input? If not, the wedge may still be too abstract.

### If someone challenges paid pilots

I get the pushback. I do not think the early price is mainly about revenue. It is about truth. A free user can be encouraging without being committed. A paying user has to decide whether the workflow matters.

### If someone says this is consulting

That is exactly the danger. I think the line is whether customer #1 teaches you something that makes customer #11 easier to sell and onboard. If every customer creates a new branch, it is consulting.

### If someone says founders should hire sales earlier

I would delay it until the founder can predict the objections and close trigger. Before that, salespeople create pipeline but filter out the learning the founder needs.
