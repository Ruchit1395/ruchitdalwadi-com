The real competitor for most vertical AI startups is not another startup.

It is the horizontal GPT tab already open in the buyer's browser.

That changes the first-customer test.

The question is not:

"Can this AI do something useful?"

The question is:

"Is this workflow-specific enough that the buyer would rather use this than ChatGPT plus a prompt?"

That usually comes down to three things:

1. Workflow context.

Does the product know the objects, constraints, edge cases, and handoff points of the workflow better than a blank chat box?

2. Reliability.

Can the buyer trust the output when the input is weird, incomplete, political, or high-stakes?

3. Handoff cost.

Does the product remove work from the team, or does it create a new review step they now have to manage?

If ChatGPT plus a prompt solves the job, there is no product yet.

The first 10 customers expose exactly where that line is.

That is why I think early B2B AI GTM is less about "selling software" and more about recruiting design partners into a workflow proof.
