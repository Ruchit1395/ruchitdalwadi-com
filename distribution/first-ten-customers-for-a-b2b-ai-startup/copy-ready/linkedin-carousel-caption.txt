The first 10 customers of a B2B AI startup are not bought with marketing.

They are recruited.

Normal B2B SaaS advice assumes the product is deterministic, the buyer knows what they want, and the demo represents reality.

Early AI products break all three assumptions.

So the first sales motion has to do two jobs at once:

1. Create revenue.
2. Create product learning.

That is why I think the first 10 customers should be founder-led design partnerships, not generic pipeline.

The uncomfortable truth:

Your first customers are paying for founder attention.

That is fine, as long as the attention turns into repeatable product learning instead of custom consulting.
