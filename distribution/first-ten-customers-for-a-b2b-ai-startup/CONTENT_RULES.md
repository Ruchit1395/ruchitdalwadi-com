# Content Rules — permanent, every post, every platform

These override everything else. Every agent generating posts, comments, or replies reads this file first. Every piece is checked against it before publishing.

## Hard bans

1. **No em dashes. Ever.** No "—" or "–" anywhere. Use a period, comma, or colon instead. This is the #1 AI-tell and the #1 rule.
2. **No repeated patterns.** Never use the same opening formula, sentence scaffold, or structure twice in a row. Banned-when-repeated scaffolds: "Most people X. But Y.", "X isn't Y. It's Z.", "Stop doing X. Start doing Y.", numbered-list-every-post, question-ending-every-post. Rotate shapes: story, checklist, hot take, war story, dry joke, counterintuitive stat, one-liner, mini case study.
3. **No hype vocabulary**: game-changer, mind-blowing, insane (as praise), revolutionary, 🚀, "excited to share".
4. **No engagement bait**: "agree?", "thoughts?", "repost if", rage bait, fake controversy.

## Voice models (study, don't copy)

- **Lenny Rachitsky**: genuinely useful frameworks, data-backed claims, humble curiosity, "here's what the data actually says". The bar: would someone save this?
- **Claire Vo**: operator candor, ship-fast energy, technically fluent, funny without trying hard. The bar: does this sound like someone who builds?
- **Aakash Gupta**: tactical depth, specific numbers, strong specific hooks, contrarian with receipts. The bar: is this specific enough that only someone who did it could write it?

Blend: **~70% insanely useful** (frameworks, playbooks, specific lessons), **~20% comic or sarcastic** (dry observational humor about AI/startup/PM life, never punching down, never cringe), **~10% conversation** (genuine questions, opinions that invite pushback).

## Audience filter (run before writing anything)

TG: founders, PMs, operators, AI builders. Busy people who ship.
The test: **would a busy founder screenshot or save this?** If it only informs but doesn't change what they do Monday, sharpen it or kill it.

## Platform nuances

**X**: punchy, concrete, first line carries everything. Short posts hit harder than long ones. Threads need a hook that creates a curiosity gap without lying. Replies: add value in the first 10 words, no throat-clearing. Humor lands well. Lowercase-casual acceptable.

**LinkedIn**: narrative and personal, short paragraphs with whitespace, professional-with-a-pulse. Story beats framework here. First 2 lines decide the "see more" click. Question endings sparingly (max 1 in 3 posts). Humor drier and rarer.

## Trends

- Ride what's live: model launches, tool releases, viral takes in the AI/PM/startup conversation. A good trend-jack post within hours beats a perfect evergreen post next week.
- The reply engine naturally rides trending rooms (Latest search, min engagement filters). For natives: when something big ships, swap the day's slot3 for a trend take.
- Never trend-jack politics, tragedy, or drama.

## Quality gate (all six must pass)

1. Zero em dashes
2. Different shape from yesterday's posts
3. A busy founder would save or laugh
4. Platform-native, not cross-posted verbatim
5. Specific over general (numbers, names of patterns, real failure modes)
6. Sounds like a person, not a content calendar
