---
title: Reducing post-capture friction for creators
domain: AI × Content editing
pillar: AI
problem: Creators have plenty of capture tools. The real bottleneck is the post-capture workflow — cutting, scoring, captioning, exporting. AI can collapse a 90-minute edit into 9 minutes if it ships in the right shape.
role: Product scoping for the AI features, plus design on the editor surface.
insight: AI-in-the-editor only works when the AI's suggestions are *commitable in one click* and *reversible in one click*. Adding confidence scores didn't matter. Adding "accept all / accept this / undo" did.
signal: Creator-tool space; mobile + desktop.
status: Shipped
updates:
  - date: 2024-11-10
    note: Shipped the first auto-cut feature with a one-click accept/reject flow.
  - date: 2025-04-20
    note: 'Replaced confidence percentages with an AI-confidence good/uncertain two-state signal. Adoption tripled.'
---

The deeper lesson here was that creators don't want AI to do more — they want AI to do *less, faster*. Anonymized for now; the patterns are generalizable across most AI-augmented creator tools.
