The 5-line task brief that makes AI coding sessions boring, in the good way.

One line each, and a known failure when the line is skipped.

1. Goal: the behavior change, one sentence. Skip it and the tool optimizes for plausible instead of intended.

2. Done means: the test or command that proves it, runnable today. Skip it and done becomes a feeling you negotiate with yourself at 6pm. This line doubles as the scope alarm: if it is hard to write, the task is too big. Split it and brief the first piece.

3. Touch: the files in scope. Anything else needs a stated reason before editing. Skip it and a rate-limit fix wanders into your auth module because something looked improvable.

4. Don't: what stays untouched. Dependencies, public APIs, config, formatting. Skip it and the diff arrives with a surprise library upgrade riding along.

5. Context: the one file or doc to read first. Skip it and the tool re-derives your conventions from scratch, wrongly, every session.

Write the brief before opening the tool, not inside it. Two honest minutes.

The failure this prevents is the expensive one: the 40-file diff for the 4-line problem. Small verified diffs merged hourly beat one heroic diff at the end of the day, and the brief is what keeps the diffs small.
