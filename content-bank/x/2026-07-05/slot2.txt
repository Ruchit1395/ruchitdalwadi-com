The agent-or-workflow test. Four questions before you build:

1. Can you list the steps in order today? If yes, it's a pipeline. Build a workflow with an LLM call inside it.

2. Does the next step depend on what the last step found? If no, you don't need dynamic planning.

3. What happens on step failure — retry, skip, or ask a human? If you can't answer, you're not ready for autonomy.

4. Who reviews runs 1 through 20? No owner, no agent.

Most "agent" projects fail question 1. The steps were knowable; someone just skipped writing them down.

A workflow that always works beats an agent that usually does — and you can upgrade a boring workflow into an agent later. Downgrading a flaky agent is a rewrite.
