Four questions before you build an agent:

1. Can you list the steps in order today? If yes, it is a pipeline. Build a workflow with an LLM call inside it.

2. Does step N+1 depend on what step N found? If no, you do not need dynamic planning.

3. What happens on failure: retry, skip, or ask a human? No answer means no autonomy yet.

4. Who reviews runs 1 through 20? No owner, no agent.

Most "agent" projects die on question 1. The steps were knowable; someone skipped writing them down. A workflow that always works beats an agent that usually does, and you can upgrade a boring workflow later. Downgrading a flaky agent is a rewrite.
