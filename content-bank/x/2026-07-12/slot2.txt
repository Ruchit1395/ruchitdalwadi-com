A practice that costs 5 minutes per incident and compounds forever — the regression diary:

Every time your AI system produces something bad enough to fix, capture three lines before moving on.

- Input: the exact case that broke it.
- Bad output: what it did.
- Rule: one sentence a checker could verify. "Stays calm on hostile input." "Never invents a ticket number." "Declines medical questions."

Once a week, turn the new lines into assertions — a regex, a small classifier, or an LLM judge checking the rule. Run the suite on every prompt change and model upgrade.

You're not measuring whether the system is good. You're measuring whether anything you already paid to fix has quietly broken again. Different question, much easier to answer — and it's the one that pages you before your users do.
