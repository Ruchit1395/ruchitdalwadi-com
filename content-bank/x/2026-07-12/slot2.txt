On an AI editing product we fixed "invents a source for statistics" three separate times before admitting the pattern. The fourth fix became a test. It has caught the same regression twice since.

The practice costs 5 minutes per incident. When the system produces something bad enough to fix, capture three lines: the exact input that broke it, what it did, and one rule a checker could verify. "Stays calm on hostile input." "Never invents a ticket number." "Declines medical questions."

Once a week, turn the new lines into assertions: a regex, a small classifier, or an LLM judge checking the rule. Run the suite on every prompt change and model upgrade.

You are not measuring whether the system is good. You are measuring whether anything you already paid to fix has quietly broken again. Much easier question, and it pages you before your users do.
