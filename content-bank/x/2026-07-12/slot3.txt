An agent resolved 32 of 34 support tickets without a human. That's >90% automation. Here's the workflow:

1.  **Ingest Ticket**: Agent receives new support ticket from email/chat.
2.  **Classify & Prioritize**: Uses an LLM to categorize the issue (bug, feature request, how-to) and assign a priority.
3.  **Search Knowledge Base**: Queries internal docs, FAQs, and past resolutions for relevant info.
4.  **Draft Response**: Generates a draft reply, often with step-by-step instructions or links to resources.
5.  **Attempt Resolution (if applicable)**: For known issues, the agent might trigger an API call to reset a password, clear a cache, or update a setting.
6.  **Human Review (for exceptions)**: Only tickets that can't be resolved or are high-priority/complex are flagged for human review. The agent provides a summary and its attempted actions.

The key is setting clear guardrails for when to escalate. Don't let the agent guess. Give it a 'no-confidence' threshold. This isn't about perfect AI, it's about offloading the predictable noise.
