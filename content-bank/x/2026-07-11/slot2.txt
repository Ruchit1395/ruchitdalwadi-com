Context engineering, minus the mystique — six slots to fill before blaming the model:

1. Goal: what does done look like, in one sentence?
2. Constraints: what must never change without asking?
3. Examples: one output you'd ship, one you'd reject. The pair teaches more than either alone.
4. Sources: which docs, tickets, or data does it read — and are they current?
5. Allowed actions: what can it do versus only suggest?
6. Review rule: what does the checker look for in 30 seconds?

When an output disappoints, walk the six slots and find the empty one. In my experience it's almost always slot 3 or slot 4 — the instructions were fine, the model was just working from a blank desk.

Fill the slots once and next week's task starts at 80% instead of zero.
