A model bake-off that takes an afternoon, not a sprint.

Write the expensive mistake first. Not "accuracy": the specific failure that costs money or trust. Refunding an ambiguous case. Inventing a citation. Deleting the wrong rows.

Build 20 test cases around that mistake: 10 where the right move is to act, 10 where the right move is to hesitate, ask, or decline.

Run every candidate on all 20. Log acts-when-it-shouldn't and hesitates-when-it-shouldn't separately. They are different costs.

Only then look at price and latency, and only among the survivors.

Most model debates are people arguing the last step on vibes without doing the first three. The leaderboard cannot know which mistake is expensive for you. That part is your job.
