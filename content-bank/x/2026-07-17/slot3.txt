One engineer 10x'd their output with Claude. The rest of the team hadn't caught up. This is the story I hear every day.

Watching teams adopt AI, I've mapped the 4 steps to scaling that initial burst of productivity:

1.  **Individual Experimentation:** A few engineers start using AI tools (like Claude) for personal tasks. They see massive gains, often 10x on specific workflows.

2.  **Informal Sharing:** Those early adopters start sharing tips, prompts, and success stories in Slack channels or during stand-ups. This sparks curiosity, but adoption is still ad-hoc.

3.  **Team-Level Integration:** A manager or tech lead identifies a common pain point that AI can solve for the whole team. They might build a shared prompt library or integrate an agent into a specific workflow.

4.  **Org-Wide Enablement:** The company invests in training, internal tooling, and clear guidelines. This moves from individual hacks to a systemic capability, turning those 10x gains into a baseline for everyone.

The biggest mistake is trying to jump straight to step 4. Start with the individual wins, then build the scaffolding.
