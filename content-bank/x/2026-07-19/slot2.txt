Product questions that matter more, not less, now that AI writes the first draft of everything:

1. What is the user's actual job? Test: what happens to them tomorrow if this feature disappears? "They would use a competitor" means you have not found it yet.

2. What does good output mean here, specifically? If the PM cannot produce 3 shippable and 3 rejectable examples, no eval can save the feature.

3. Which mistake is expensive? Wrong tone is survivable; wrong refund is not. This ranking drives model choice, review depth, and rollout speed.

4. Where does the human stay in the loop, and until when? "Forever" and "nowhere" are both wrong answers. Name the graduation criteria.

The demo is a week of work now. These four questions are the quarter that follows, and they are the part AI cannot do for you.
