The demo now takes a week. The judgment still takes the quarter.

Blank canvas to working prototype collapsed from months to days, and that fooled a lot of roadmaps into thinking the product work collapsed with it. It did not. It moved. Everything after the demo is where the job lives now: defining what good output means, ranking which mistake is expensive, deciding where a human stays in the loop and what graduates them out.

None of that got faster, because none of it was ever a typing problem. It is taste, context, and consequences, and AI gives it a faster feedback loop rather than a substitute.

The teams shipping real AI products did not skip the judgment. They just stopped spending the quarter on the part that now takes a week.
