Every AI feels like a "yes man" right now. You ask for feedback, and it claps. This isn't helpful when you're trying to build something great.

To get real critique, you need to engineer an AI with a spine. Here's how to set up a "critic" skill:

1.  **Create a dedicated markdown file.** Its sole job is to attack the work. It hunts for the weakest claim, the dodged question, the thing you're ignoring.
2.  **Give it specific instructions.** Tell it to find logical fallacies, weak arguments, and areas where the writing lacks conviction. It should be relentless.
3.  **Integrate it into your workflow.** Before you finalize anything, run it through your AI critic. Treat its feedback like a tough but fair editor.

This isn't about getting praise. It's about getting better. A critical AI helps you find the actual flaws before your users do.
