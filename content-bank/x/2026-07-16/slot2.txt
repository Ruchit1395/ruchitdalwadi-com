Silent failures worth instrumenting before they find you — the change monitor:

1. Length drift: average response length moves 20% with no deploy. Something upstream changed.

2. Hedge rate: count "it depends", "generally", "may vary" per 100 outputs. Rising hedges often mean degraded context or a source went missing.

3. Refusal rate: sudden politeness spikes usually trace to a prompt or policy change, not to user behavior.

4. Empty-input passthrough: a retrieval step returns nothing and the model improvises confidently. Log source token counts per request; alert on zero.

5. Format drift: the JSON still parses, but a field went from sentences to fragments.

None of these requires knowing what correct means. Sample 1% of traffic, track weekly averages, alert on movement. Cheap to build — and it catches the failures nobody reports because every individual output still looks fine.
