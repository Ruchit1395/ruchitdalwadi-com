Silent failures worth instrumenting before they find you:

Length drift. Average response length moves 20% with no deploy. Something upstream changed.

Hedge rate. Count "it depends", "generally", "may vary" per 100 outputs. Rising hedges often mean degraded context or a missing source.

Refusal rate. Sudden politeness spikes usually trace to a prompt or policy change, not to user behavior.

Empty-input passthrough. A retrieval step returns nothing and the model improvises confidently. Log source token counts per request; alert on zero.

Format drift. The JSON still parses, but a field went from sentences to fragments.

None of these requires knowing what correct means. Sample 1% of traffic, track weekly averages, alert on movement. It catches the failures nobody reports because every individual output still looks fine.
