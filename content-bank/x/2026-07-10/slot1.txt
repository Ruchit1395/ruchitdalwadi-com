A stop condition is the difference between reviewing decisions and reviewing surprises.

Watching a logistics ops agent through its first month made the economics obvious. The runs that halted early and asked a human cost us minutes each. The one run that barreled through a missing customs field cost a week of cleanup and a very awkward customer call. Same agent, same tools. The only variable was whether it knew when to stop.

Teams write pages about what an agent should do and one vague line about when it should not. Reverse that ratio for a day. Name the exact states that trigger a halt: required input missing, confidence low, action irreversible, cost above a number someone chose on purpose.

An agent that stops well feels slower in the demo and much faster in the quarter.
