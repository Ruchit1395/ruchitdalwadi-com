The most important line in an agent spec is the one about giving up.

Everyone specs what the agent should do. Almost nobody specs when it should stop — and that's the line that decides whether your team reviews decisions or gets surprised by them.

A stop condition is concrete: halt when a required input is missing, when the action can't be undone, when the cost passes a threshold, when confidence drops below the bar. And the handoff at that moment is a designed artifact: what I tried, what I found, what's blocking me, what I'd do next. Four sentences a human can act on in a minute.

I'd trade ten points of raw capability for a reliable "I'm stuck because X is missing." The first is impressive in a demo. The second is what lets you actually delegate.

An agent without a stop condition isn't autonomous. It just has momentum.
