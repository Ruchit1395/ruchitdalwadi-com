Everyone specs what the agent should do. Almost nobody specs when it should stop.

That missing line decides whether your team reviews decisions or gets surprised by them. A stop condition is concrete: halt when a required input is missing, when the action cannot be undone, when cost passes a threshold, when confidence drops below the bar.

The handoff at that moment is a designed artifact too: what I tried, what I found, what is blocking me, what I would do next. Four sentences a human can act on in a minute.

Watching a logistics ops agent run its first month taught me the ratio. The runs that stopped early and asked cost us minutes. The one run that barreled through a missing customs field cost a week of cleanup and a very awkward customer call.

I would trade ten points of raw capability for a reliable "I am stuck because X is missing." The first is impressive in a demo. The second is what lets you actually delegate.

An agent without a stop condition is not autonomous. It just has momentum.
