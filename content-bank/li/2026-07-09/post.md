"Which model should we use?" is usually asked backwards.

The leaderboard can tell you which model writes the smartest average answer. It can't tell you which mistake would be expensive for your business — and that's the deciding variable.

For a support workflow, the expensive mistake might be refunding an ambiguous case. For research, a confident invented citation. For a coding agent, editing a file nobody asked it to touch. Different mistakes, different winners — sometimes the "weaker" model wins because it hesitates in exactly the right places.

The eval that settles the debate is written in business language, not benchmark language: does it ask the right clarifying question, cite the right policy, hand off cleanly when unsure? Twenty test cases built around your costliest failure will beat any leaderboard screenshot.
