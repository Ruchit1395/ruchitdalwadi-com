The demo now takes a week. The product job starts after the demo.

That's the biggest shift AI made to product work, and it's widely misread as "PMs matter less now." The opposite happened. When blank-canvas-to-working-prototype took two quarters, product judgment had months to catch up. Now the prototype exists on day five, and every unanswered product question becomes a production incident instead of a planning discussion.

What does a good output look like, specifically — can you show three you'd ship and three you'd reject? Which mistake is expensive and which is survivable? Who reviews outputs, and what graduates the feature out of review?

Those aren't engineering questions. They're the oldest product questions there are — evaluation criteria, risk, trust — arriving faster than roadmaps used to.

AI compressed the distance from idea to demo. What's your process for the distance from demo to trusted?
