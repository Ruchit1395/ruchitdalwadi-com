The engineers getting the most out of AI coding tools aren't better prompters. They're better scopers.

Watch one of them work. Before touching the tool, they've written the goal in a sentence, named the command that proves it works, listed which files are in bounds, and said out loud what must not change. The AI session that follows looks almost boring — small task in, small verified diff out, repeat.

Now watch a frustrated team: "refactor the service layer" typed into a chat box, followed by a 40-file diff nobody wants to review, followed by "this tool isn't ready."

Same tool. The difference is the scoping work — which, notably, is the same skill that made people good at delegating to junior engineers. The tools reward it brutally.
