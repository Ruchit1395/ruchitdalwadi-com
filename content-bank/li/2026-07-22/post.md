I’ve seen a pattern emerge with LLMs that feels counterintuitive but works: the "ramble session." 

When you’re trying to get a complex output from an LLM and it keeps missing the mark, the instinct is to refine your prompt. But sometimes, the model just needs more *context* than you’re willing to type out. 

The solution: switch to voice input and just talk. Ramble for five to ten minutes, stream-of-consciousness. Don't worry about structure or typos. Just dump every thought, every nuance, every related idea into the input. 

This provides the LLM with a much richer, albeit messier, dataset to draw from. It's like giving it a full conversation transcript rather than a bulleted summary. The model can then extract the signal from your raw thoughts. 

I'd use this for tasks where precision is key but the initial framing is hard to articulate concisely. It's a low-effort way to inject high-fidelity context.
