The most valuable AI asset at your company might be sitting in the trash.

Every rejected AI draft — the summary that missed the point, the reply that cited the wrong policy — carries exact information about what your system is missing. What context wasn't provided. Which judgment call the model shouldn't have made alone. What the reviewer checked in the 30 seconds before saying no.

Teams delete these and go looking for better prompts.

Try the opposite for one week. Keep every reject, with a one-line reason. On Friday, read them together. You'll find the same three gaps repeating — usually a stale source doc, a missing example of what good looks like, and a decision that should have stayed human.

Fix those three and you haven't improved a prompt. You've built a system that makes every future prompt work better.

Your rejects are your requirements. Stop throwing away the spec.
