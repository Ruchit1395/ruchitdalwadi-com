Prompt libraries age like bread. Systems age like cast iron.

Here's the difference in practice. A prompt is the sentence you type. A system is everything around it: the context the model gets by default, the two examples it imitates, the tools it may call, the check that runs before output ships, and the person who owns exceptions.

When a prompt fails, you tweak words and hope. When a system fails, you can point at the broken part — missing context, weak example, no review step — and fix that part.

One upgrade to start with: take your most-used prompt and attach one gold example of an output you'd ship and one you'd reject. That single pair usually improves results more than a paragraph of extra instructions.

The prompt is the smallest part of the system — and often the only part a team has actually built.
